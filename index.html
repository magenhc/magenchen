ABOUT ME

I am a sixteen-year-old student from Toronto, Canada, who is passionate about music. I am an active member in my school community, taking part in several clubs such as Amnesty International, Peer Tutor, Relay for Life Committee, Science Olympics, Musical Pit Orchestra and Carter Cantabile Strings. As a rising senior, I look forward to applying to colleges this year. This summer, I am learning to code at the Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program.

My hobbies include reading literature, writing, videography, and photography.

Music

At a young age, I began playing the violin and piano. When I was ten years old, I became a violinist in the Canadian Sinfonietta Youth Orchestra (CSYO).

In 2015, I was admitted to an arts-focused high school in Toronto, Cardinal Carter Academy for the Arts, which I currently attend. At school, I am an active member of Arts for the Benefit of Others (ABO), where I performed solo and in an ensemble for senior homes and church services a few times a year. In 2018, I was the second violinist of the pit orchestra in my school's musical production, "Joseph and the Technicolor Dreamcoat."

In Novemeber 2017, I was part of an ensemble of 11th-grade students at Cardinal Carter. The video below is our performance of Ruth B.'s "Lost Boy" for string quartet and piano, arranged by our pianist Mark Amirthanathan. We had lots of fun pitching ideas to polish the piece and practicing together to achieve the sound and story we wanted to portray. We were one of the top 10 finalists for the High School Instrumental Category of the Canadian Music Class Challenge of 2017.
